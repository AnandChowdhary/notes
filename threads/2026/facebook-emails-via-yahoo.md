It ignores consent, depends on flaky third-party APIs, and blurs why data was collected in the first place. Classic "growth hack with the social graph" thinking. But the instinct behind it still holds up. Founders in India still do not have a portable, protocol-level identity that is not locked into LinkedIn or whichever platform is trending. If I rebuilt MWLII now, I would flip it: user-authored, verifiable profiles, protocol-first, no hidden scraping. Less "how can we pull this data" and more "how can founders truly own and carry their identity across tools".
